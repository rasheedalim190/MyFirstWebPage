The Best Movies According to Rasheed Alim

My top 10 movies/series of all-time.

Avengers: Endgame

Following the catastrophic loss in Infinity War, the remaining heroes embark on a daring mission to reverse Thanos’ snap by traveling through time and confronting their past. The film delivers emotional farewells, heroic sacrifices, and an epic final battle that unites nearly every Marvel character, making it both a thrilling spectacle and a heartfelt conclusion to over a decade of interconnected storytelling.

Avengers: Infinity War

The Avengers and their allies face their most formidable foe yet as Thanos seeks to collect all six Infinity Stones to wield ultimate power. The film balances multiple storylines across the Marvel universe, uniting heroes from Earth and space in a desperate fight to stop him. With breathtaking action, shocking twists, and a devastating ending that sees half of all life erased, it stands out as an epic chapter for its scale, emotional impact, and bold willingness to leave audiences stunned and eager for the conclusion.

Spider-Man: No Way Home

After his identity is revealed, Peter Parker seeks help from Doctor Strange to restore his secret identity. However, the spell goes wrong and opens portals to other dimensions, bringing villains from different universes. The film combines action, humor, and emotional depth as Peter deals with the consequences of his actions while facing threats from across the multiverse.

Black Panther

T'Challa returns to Wakanda to take his place as king but faces a challenge from his cousin, Erik Killmonger, who seeks to overthrow him and restore the kingdom's former glory. The film explores themes of identity, heritage, and justice while delivering stunning visuals and a powerful message about the importance of protecting one's people and values.

Inside Out 2

Inside Out 2 follows the emotional journey of Riley as she navigates the complexities of adolescence, with her emotions now joined by new feelings like anxiety and excitement. The film explores how these evolving emotions shape her identity and relationships, offering a deeper look into the inner workings of the mind.

WandaVision

Wanda Maximoff and Vision's life in the suburbs of Westview is disrupted when they are forced to confront the truth about their reality. The series explores themes of identity, power, and the consequences of magic, while delivering a unique blend of humor and horror.

Stranger Things Series

Set in the 1980s, this sci‑fi horror series follows a group of kids in Hawkins, Indiana, as they uncover government experiments and battle supernatural forces from the mysterious Upside Down. Blending nostalgic pop culture references with gripping suspense, heartfelt friendships, and terrifying monsters, the show is epic for its ability to mix coming‑of‑age drama with high‑stakes adventure, creating a cultural phenomenon that resonates with both longtime fans of retro storytelling and new audiences drawn to its emotional depth and thrilling mysteries.

The Guardians of The Galaxy

A group of intergalactic misfits, including Peter Quill, Gamora, Drax, Rocket, and Groot, come together to protect a powerful orb from falling into the hands of the villainous Ronan the Accuser. The film combines action, humor, and a nostalgic soundtrack to create a unique space adventure that emphasizes the importance of friendship and teamwork.

Thor: Ragnarok

Thor is sent to the planet Sakaar, where he is forced to fight in a gladiator arena. He teams up with the Hulk and other warriors to escape and stop the destruction of Asgard. The film combines action, humor, and a vibrant visual style to create a unique take on the Marvel universe.

Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness

Doctor Strange teams up with Wanda Maximoff to navigate the multiverse and confront a new threat that endangers all realities. The film explores themes of power, responsibility, and the consequences of tampering with reality, while delivering mind-bending visuals and intense action sequences.
